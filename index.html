The Creator of Dragon Ball

Akira Toriyama

Akira Toriyama was a legendary Japanese manga artist best known for creating Dragon Ball, one of the most influential manga and anime series of all time. His unique art style and storytelling revolutionized the shōnen genre, inspiring generations of creators.

Toriyama's character designs extended beyond manga, influencing popular video games like Dragon Quest and Chrono Trigger.

About Akira

Early Career & Breakthrough (1978–1984)

After quitting his job as a graphic designer, Toriyama debuted with Wonder Island in Weekly Shōnen Jump. His first major success, Dr. Slump (1980), became a hit manga and anime, earning him the Shogakukan Manga Award.

The Birth of Dragon Ball (1984–1995)

In 1984, he created Dragon Ball, blending martial arts, adventure, and humor, which quickly became a cultural phenomenon. The series evolved into Dragon Ball Z (1989), introducing legendary battles and shaping the modern shōnen genre.

Expanding His Legacy (1995–2012)

After concluding Dragon Ball in 1995, Toriyama explored new projects, including Sand Land and Cowa!, while designing for games like Dragon Quest and Chrono Trigger. Though Dragon Ball GT aired during this time, he had minimal involvement.

Revival of Dragon Ball (2013–2023)

Toriyama revived the franchise with Dragon Ball Z: Battle of Gods (2013), bringing new transformations and lore. He continued shaping the series with Dragon Ball Super (2015) and movies like Broly (2018) and Super Hero (2022).

Final Works & Legacy (2023–2024)

His classic Sand Land received a new anime and game adaptation, showing his influence beyond Dragon Ball. On March 1, 2024, Akira Toriyama passed away, leaving behind a legendary legacy in manga, anime, and gaming.
